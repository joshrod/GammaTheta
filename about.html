About Us

Lambda Theta Phi Latin Fraternity Incorporated was founded on December 1st, 1975 at Kean University in Union, New Jersey. Fourteen visionary gentlemen pioneered the institution of the Latino Fraternity, and ushered in the era of the Latino Greek movement, which was established as a response to the need for unity within the different Latin American diasporic communities in the United States. Recognizing Latinos’ lack of representation in campus leadership circles, these gentlemen sought to create an organization that would foster academic excellence, brotherhood and service, while reaffirming a commitment to preserving cultural identity. We made history as the first nationally recognized Latino Fraternity, and we continue to honor this legacy by developing new generations of campus and community leaders.

On April 1st, 2006, Lambda Theta Phi became the first Latino fraternity established at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. Since our inception on campus, our mission has been to create an environment where the Latino college student can be part of a family with a mission. Our brothers perform community service, promote our culture by sponsoring events which highlight and celebrate our heritage, and participate in educational workshops. As a result of their active participation in every aspect of college life, our undergraduates are developing their organizational, communication, and leadership skills, all while pursuing that coveted diploma. Though we are historically Latino based, we are not solely a Latino Fraternity. We have Brothers of all different races, religions, national origins, sexual orientations and gender identities, rooted in our commitment to the ideal that in unity there is strength― “En la Unión Está la Fuerza”."
